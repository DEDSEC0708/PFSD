Diwali Festival

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is one of the most significant Hindu festivals in India. It is celebrated over five days and signifies the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

During Diwali, people clean and decorate their homes with oil lamps (diyas) and colorful rangoli designs. Exchanging gifts and sharing delicious sweets and savory dishes are common customs. Families come together for prayers, and the night sky is illuminated with fireworks.

Lohri Festival

Lohri Festival

Lohri is a Punjabi winter festival celebrated with great enthusiasm. It signifies the end of winter and the arrival of longer, warmer days.

People gather around bonfires, offer prayers, and toss offerings like popcorn, sesame seeds, and jaggery into the fire to seek blessings. They sing traditional folk songs and engage in lively dances like the Bhangra, showcasing the spirit of celebration.

Traditional Punjabi foods such as sesame seed sweets and rewri are enjoyed during this festive occasion.

Maha Shivaratri Festival

Maha Shivaratri Festival

Maha Shivaratri is a significant Hindu festival dedicated to Lord Shiva, the destroyer of evil. Devotees observe fasts, visit Shiva temples, and perform religious rituals with deep devotion.

It is believed that observing Maha Shivaratri with sincerity purifies the soul and brings blessings. Temples are adorned with flowers, and devotees offer milk, water, and Bilva leaves to the Shiva Lingam, seeking Lord Shiva's grace.
